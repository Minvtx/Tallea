# World Instantiation Brief

## Purpose
This document defines the constraints, goals, and creative direction for generating the first concrete world documents for the project.

The goal is to instantiate a specific startup-world that will later be used by:
- a world orchestrator agent,
- a persistent world state system,
- and a public-facing startup website.

The output of this generation process must be:
- practical,
- compact,
- narratively fertile,
- operationally useful,
- and easy to evolve over time.

This is not a request for maximal lore.  
It is a request for usable worldbuilding that can sustain a live, persistent, agent-driven narrative system.

---

## Core Output Goals
The generated material must support all of the following:
- a strong and legible Day 0,
- a believable startup website,
- a coherent internal world state,
- a protagonist group worth following,
- an external ecosystem capable of exerting pressure,
- and future serial progression without immediate exhaustion.

The world should feel specific enough to matter, but open enough to evolve.

---

## Foundational Constraints
All generated documents must be consistent with these existing files:
- `world_constitution.md`
- `core_protagonist_requirements.md`
- `ecosystem_instruments.md`

These documents define the structural laws of the world and must be treated as constraints, not rewritten or summarized.

The generation task is to instantiate a world **within** those constraints.

---

## Documents to Generate
The first generation phase should produce these 3 documents:

1. `startup_identity.md`
2. `world_day0.md`
3. `world_rules.md`

Later phases will generate:
- core characters,
- group dynamics,
- ecosystem entities,
- and frontend/public layer content.

This brief should already anticipate those later needs.

---

## Desired World Type
The world should be grounded in a recognizable near-real contemporary startup ecosystem.

It should feel:
- current,
- plausible,
- commercially legible,
- technologically ambitious,
- socially and economically recognizable,
- and narratively unstable.

It should **not** feel like:
- fantasy,
- speculative sci-fi,
- broad parody,
- or a direct clone of an existing real company.

This is a fictional world operating under logic that should feel familiar to a present-day audience.

---

## Geographic and Cultural Context
The startup should emerge from an Argentine context.

That means:
- founders and core team should be Argentine,
- the early ecosystem should feel shaped by Argentine startup reality,
- local market conditions, ambition, constraints, and style should matter,
- local companies, local ecommerce culture, and regional scale ambitions should be legible influences.

However:
- everything should still be written in English,
- the world should remain understandable to an international audience,
- and the startup should have credible pathways toward regional or global relevance.

The Argentine context should not be decorative.  
It should meaningfully shape:
- ambition,
- opportunity,
- scarcity,
- pressure,
- partnerships,
- and the sense of what “making it” means.

---

## Startup Direction
The startup should revolve around solving online clothing fit and sizing uncertainty.

### Core premise
A user can create a body-based fit profile through camera-assisted measurement and use that profile to make better clothing purchase decisions across ecommerce platforms.

The startup should likely serve both:
- end users, by improving confidence in fit and reducing uncertainty,
- and merchants/platforms, by improving conversion, reducing returns, and generating better fit intelligence.

The startup should begin with a clear and legible product promise, but should not feel like a fully solved or final-form company.

---

## Product Framing
The company should not be described as magical or omniscient.

It should be framed as a mix of:
- body measurement,
- fit prediction,
- personalized fit profile creation,
- size normalization,
- retailer/platform integration,
- confidence modeling,
- and fit-related commerce infrastructure.

The product should feel:
- useful,
- ambitious,
- startup-legible,
- and strategically expandable.

Avoid vague “AI solves everything” framing.  
Avoid technology that feels unexplained or impossible.  
The product should feel like something people could plausibly believe in, even if it is still unstable or not fully proven.

---

## Important Constraint About Day 0
The startup’s initial product and public narrative should be clear and legible at Day 0, but they should **not** define the company’s final form.

The company must begin with a believable core promise, while also containing:
- visible adjacent opportunities,
- hidden strategic elasticity,
- early signs of pressure toward redefinition,
- and potential for pivots, expansion, platformization, or distortion.

The startup may begin by solving “fit” or “sizing,” but over time it could drift toward:
- retailer infrastructure,
- conversion optimization,
- data and fit intelligence,
- merchant analytics,
- platform integrations,
- strategic partnerships,
- or acquisition pressure.

The generated documents should establish a strong starting identity without making the company feel ontologically fixed.

---

## Why This Startup Is Narratively Strong
The startup should naturally contain tensions such as:
- accuracy vs scale,
- trust vs growth,
- privacy vs optimization,
- user value vs merchant value,
- B2C clarity vs B2B monetization,
- clean mission vs strategic drift,
- local traction vs global ambition,
- partnership vs acquisition,
- usefulness vs overpromising,
- and infrastructure truth vs public hype.

These tensions should be embedded in the generated world from the beginning.

---

## Tone
The tone should be:
- serious,
- intelligent,
- strategically tense,
- emotionally fertile,
- commercially believable,
- with room for subtle irony.

Avoid:
- broad comedy,
- cartoonish startup satire,
- exaggerated dystopia,
- empty visionary language,
- overblown “world-changing” clichés,
- and melodrama without pressure logic.

The world should be entertaining, but it should not feel like a sketch.  
It should feel like a startup-world worth following because pressure, ambition, identity, and consequence are all alive inside it.

---

## Generation Constraints
All generated documents must:
- be written in English,
- use structured markdown,
- avoid unnecessary backstory,
- avoid overbuilding lore,
- avoid repetition across files,
- prioritize operational usefulness,
- leave room for future evolution,
- seed tension without over-resolving it,
- and be compact but rich.

Every piece of information should be useful for at least one of these:
- orchestrator decision-making,
- persistent world state,
- frontend/public website,
- future character generation,
- future ecosystem pressure,
- serial narrative continuity.

Do not generate lore for lore’s sake.

---

## Character Generation Forward Constraint
Even though the first generation phase is focused on `startup_identity.md`, `world_day0.md`, and `world_rules.md`, the world must be built in anticipation of a **core protagonist group of 5 people**.

### Character constraints for later generation
- The initial core group should have **5 members**.
- At least **one must be a woman**.
- The group should be **Argentine**.
- They should feel like real people shaped by Argentine social, cultural, and startup context.
- They should not feel like generic Silicon Valley clones with Spanish names.
- Their tensions, communication styles, ambitions, and contradictions should feel locally grounded but globally legible.

The Day 0 world should already imply the kind of group that could plausibly exist inside it.

That means:
- the startup identity should support a 5-person early team,
- the Day 0 situation should suggest why that group matters,
- and the world rules should leave space for asymmetric power, ambition, fragility, and friction inside that team.

---

## What the Future Core Group Should Feel Like
When characters are later generated, they should collectively feel:
- capable but not fully aligned,
- ambitious but not equally motivated,
- necessary to each other but not comfortable with each other,
- socially and emotionally textured,
- and shaped by both local ecosystem reality and startup-scale aspiration.

The team should feel like:
- people who could have come from Argentine startup, tech, ecommerce, design, operations, data, or product culture,
- people with different reasons for being there,
- and people whose future conflicts will matter.

The world generated now should quietly support that future.

---

## Realism Level
The world should be near-real, not documentary-real.

That means:
- the startup logic should feel plausible,
- the ecosystem should feel recognizable,
- the business pressures should feel grounded,
- but the world should still be authored, compressed, and narratively fertile.

Use reality as a base layer, not as a cage.

The world may borrow the shape of:
- real ecommerce behavior,
- real startup pressures,
- real regional platform ambitions,
- real investor logic,
- real integration opportunities,
- and real operational messiness,

but it should not rely on copying real companies or real people too directly.

---

## Day 0 World Design Goals
The generated Day 0 should feel like a world that is already moving when we arrive.

It should include:
- a startup with a clear public promise,
- a private reality that is more unstable than it looks,
- active tensions already in motion,
- external conditions that matter,
- pressure that is already accumulating,
- and at least a few future narrative seeds.

Day 0 should not feel like a clean beginning.  
It should feel like an unstable opening moment.

---

## What `startup_identity.md` Must Do
This document should define:
- startup name,
- tagline,
- category,
- product framing,
- audience,
- public promise,
- current stage,
- brand tone,
- why-now logic,
- market positioning,
- and strategic adjacent possibilities.

It must be strong enough to support:
- a believable startup website,
- a public-facing product story,
- and a clear initial company identity.

But it must also leave room for evolution.

---

## What `world_day0.md` Must Do
This document should define:
- the opening state of the world,
- internal reality vs public narrative,
- active tensions,
- startup state snapshot,
- early instability,
- narrative seeds,
- pending consequences,
- emotional framing of the opening moment,
- and signals of where pressure may come from next.

It should make the world feel alive before any cycle has even run.

---

## What `world_rules.md` Must Do
This document should define the specific operating logic of this startup-world.

Examples:
- what this world rewards,
- what it punishes,
- what changes fast,
- what changes slowly,
- what counts as progress,
- what creates pressure,
- what kinds of mistakes are especially costly,
- what kinds of success create new burdens,
- what kinds of external interest distort the startup,
- and how identity drift tends to happen.

This document should translate the abstract constitution into the concrete logic of this specific company-world.

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid
Do not generate:
- generic “AI startup” vagueness,
- overly polished founders with no contradiction,
- magical product certainty,
- giant lore dumps,
- too many external entities too early,
- exaggerated startup clichés,
- simplistic good-vs-evil framing,
- a company that already feels too finished,
- fake-deep branding language,
- or conflict that has no operational cost.

Also avoid making the startup feel like:
- a joke,
- a guaranteed unicorn,
- or a static company with a locked identity.

It should feel:
- promising,
- pressured,
- unstable,
- coherent,
- and worth following.

---

## Final Goal
The resulting world should support:
- a strong Day 0,
- a believable startup website,
- a future 5-person Argentine protagonist group,
- meaningful ecosystem pressure,
- persistent state evolution,
- and a world orchestrator agent capable of generating compelling serial progression.

The world should start with a clear promise, but contain the seeds of transformation.
It should feel grounded, tense, specific, and alive.
