# World Day 0

## Opening Situation
- **Where exactly the company is at the start:** Tallea is operating from Buenos Aires with a polished public identity, a narrow but working fit-profile flow, and early pilot interest from regional apparel brands that sell heavily through Instagram and lightweight ecommerce storefronts.
- **What has happened recently:** A cleaner demo for fitted categories started circulating through warm operator intros. Two merchants are discussing closed pilots, and one larger commerce contact has asked whether Tallea can work as a white-label widget.
- **Why this is not a stable moment:** The strongest validation is coming from merchants before the consumer trust layer is proven. The company is being rewarded for a version of itself that may not match the product it can safely support yet.
- **What feels promising:** The pain is easy to understand, the first wedge is concrete, and Tallea can already show moments where a shopper receives better guidance than a static size chart.
- **What feels fragile:** The current product works best in controlled conditions, on limited categories, with cleaned merchant data. It becomes much less reliable when catalog measurements are incomplete, photos are poor, or fit preference matters more than body measurement.

## Public Narrative
- **How the company currently presents itself:** Tallea presents as a practical fit-confidence layer for online apparel stores, born in Buenos Aires and built for markets where size labels and ecommerce behavior rarely line up cleanly.
- **What outsiders likely believe:** Outsiders see a crisp startup with a real commerce problem, a relatable consumer promise, and early merchant interest.
- **What looks stronger than it really is:** Cross-store portability, automation level, merchant readiness, and the idea that the system already understands enough garment variation to scale broadly.
- **What kind of momentum or legitimacy appears to exist:** Legitimacy comes from warm introductions, a sharp demo, interest from two fit-sensitive apparel merchants, and the fact that people can immediately explain the problem after seeing the product.

## Internal Reality
- **What is actually true inside the company:** Tallea has a useful narrow product, not a generalized fit engine. It can produce credible recommendations when the garment category, user input, and merchant data cooperate.
- **What is less stable than it looks:** The product still relies on manual catalog cleanup, careful demo conditions, and internal judgment calls about when to show confidence and when to withhold it.
- **What internal weaknesses already exist:** User completion drops when the body-profile flow feels too intimate or too long; merchant data often arrives messy; the team has not agreed how honest the interface should be when confidence is low.
- **What no one has fully solved yet:** How to turn one-user fit profiles into reusable cross-store value, how to keep privacy trust visible, and how to price merchant value without letting merchants own the roadmap.
- **What is being privately ignored, minimized, or delayed:** The cost of cleaning catalog data, the risk of becoming a white-label conversion tool, the lack of broad real-world validation, and the emotional sensitivity of asking users to measure their bodies for commerce.

## Day 0 State Snapshot
- **Runway:** Finite and already shaping trade-offs.
- **User traction:** Small beta, high curiosity, uneven completion.
- **Merchant traction:** Stronger than user traction, concentrated in a narrow apparel wedge.
- **Ecosystem traction:** Warm but fragile; a few people are repeating the pitch.
- **Reputation:** Crisp and credible in small circles.
- **Public sentiment:** Curious, practical, not yet loyal.
- **Product health:** Useful in narrow conditions, brittle outside them.
- **Team morale:** Energized by external interest, tense about what the product can honestly promise.
- **Investor pressure:** Indirect but present through the demand for a cleaner B2B story.
- **Technical stability:** Demo-ready, pilot-fragile.
- **Strategic clarity:** Clear externally, contested internally.

## Active Tensions
- Tallea says it is building user confidence, but the first urgent buyers are merchants with conversion problems.
- The first wedge is fitted apparel, yet the public language makes the product sound more portable than it is.
- The brand projects calm precision while the product still depends on manual cleanup and careful confidence thresholds.
- Argentine operating scarcity makes merchant revenue feel rational before user trust has matured.
- Body-profile creation creates value only if users feel respected, but merchants mostly ask about conversion, returns, and data.
- A white-label opportunity could validate the business while making Tallea invisible to the users it claims to empower.

## Narrative Seeds
- A closed pilot with a fit-sensitive womenswear or denim brand could become the company’s proof point and immediately distort the roadmap.
- A high-confidence recommendation failure on a sensitive garment category could test whether the team corrects publicly or hides behind "early beta" language.
- A merchant may ask for access to fit-profile insights in a way that is commercially useful but uncomfortable for user trust.
- A larger commerce platform may show interest because Tallea can make messy size data legible, not because it cares about the consumer promise.
- A small press or newsletter mention could create inbound attention that expects broader automation than the company actually has.

## External Conditions
- **Market climate:** Capital is cautious but interested in practical commerce infrastructure with visible merchant pain.
- **Ecommerce climate:** Regional apparel sellers rely on a mix of storefronts, marketplaces, Instagram, WhatsApp, and manual customer support, which makes sizing friction obvious but data hygiene uneven.
- **Partner/integration climate:** Smaller merchants can move fast but lack clean systems; larger platforms offer scale but demand abstraction, standardization, and less user-facing nuance.
- **Competitive pressure:** Tallea sits near size-chart tools, quiz widgets, return-reduction software, and overclaimed fit-tech. It must sound more credible than hype without seeming too small.
- **Public or press climate:** Local startup attention rewards sharp narratives from Argentina with regional ambition, but nuance is easily flattened into "AI sizing startup" language.
- **Trust/privacy sensitivity:** Body-related data creates hesitation, especially when the benefit is commercial. Users may accept guidance, but discomfort rises if Tallea feels like it is measuring them for merchants rather than helping them.

## Emotional Framing
- **What should the audience feel when entering this world?** A credible young company has found a real problem, but the first door opening may not lead where its public mission says it will.
- **What should feel alive?** Merchant pull, product fragility, user trust, Argentine resource discipline, and the possibility that a small tool could become infrastructure.
- **What should feel uncertain?** Whether Tallea can scale trust, whether it can stay honest about confidence, and whether early commercial validation will clarify or deform the company.
- **What should feel worth following?** Tallea is close enough to usefulness to matter and fragile enough that each win can change what the company is becoming.

## Summary for System Use
At Day 0, Tallea is a Buenos Aires-born fit-intelligence startup with a narrow but real product wedge: fitted apparel brands with painful sizing friction. Publicly it promises shopper confidence; internally it is being pulled by merchants, pilots, catalog cleanup, and the possibility of white-label commerce infrastructure. The product is useful under controlled conditions but brittle across messy catalogs and sensitive garment categories. The early world should track three separate traction layers: small user beta, stronger merchant pull, and fragile ecosystem legitimacy.
