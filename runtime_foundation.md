# Runtime Foundation

## Purpose
This is the compact runtime brief for orchestrator agents.
Use this document before generating Day 1 or future cycles.
Query longer lore files only when a cycle needs deeper detail.

## Startup Core
- **Company:** Tallea
- **Origin:** Buenos Aires, Argentina
- **Category:** Fit intelligence for online apparel commerce
- **Public promise:** Help shoppers know likely fit before buying online.
- **Actual Day 0 pressure:** Merchant demand is stronger than user traction, and the product is narrower than the public story implies.

## Initial Wedge
- **First market:** Argentine and regional direct-to-consumer apparel brands.
- **Best early category:** Fitted womenswear and denim-adjacent products.
- **Merchant pain:** High sizing questions, abandoned carts, avoidable exchanges, messy support workflows.
- **Distribution reality:** Instagram-led discovery, WhatsApp/manual support, lightweight storefronts, uneven catalog data.

## Product Boundary
- **Reliable now:** Guided phone-camera profile, basic body measurement estimation, preference capture, confidence guidance for narrow fitted categories with clean merchant data.
- **Inconsistent now:** Denim, trousers, stretch fabrics, oversized cuts, vanity sizing, incomplete catalogs, measurements trapped in images/PDFs/support memory.
- **Not available now:** Universal size prediction, true virtual try-on, reliable cross-brand comparison, automated catalog cleanup at scale, all-body/all-category confidence.
- **Runtime rule:** Tallea may recommend with confidence ranges; it must not promise certainty.

## Public vs Internal Reality
- **Public narrative:** Practical fit-confidence layer for online apparel.
- **Internal reality:** Narrow product, manual catalog cleanup, careful demo conditions, unresolved strategic identity.
- **Main gap:** The website can sound cleaner than the system is.
- **Core risk:** Success may push Tallea toward merchant infrastructure before user trust is earned.

## Active State at Day 0
- **Runway:** Finite; already shaping decisions.
- **User traction:** Small beta, high curiosity, uneven completion.
- **Merchant traction:** Stronger than user traction; concentrated in fitted-apparel pilots.
- **Ecosystem traction:** Warm but fragile; a few operators are repeating the pitch.
- **Product health:** Useful in narrow conditions, brittle outside them.
- **Technical stability:** Demo-ready, pilot-fragile.
- **Strategic clarity:** Clear externally, contested internally.

## Main Internal Fault Lines
- Shopper trust vs merchant revenue
- Confidence range vs sales clarity
- Automation story vs manual cleanup
- Local wedge vs global ambition
- Privacy friction vs onboarding completion
- Pilot speed vs product boundary

## Pressure Priorities for Early Cycles
1. Merchant pilot pressure
2. Catalog-data mess
3. Body-profile trust and completion
4. Public claim vs product truth
5. White-label/infrastructure temptation
6. Runway pressure

## Always Preserve
- Tallea is useful but not magical.
- The product works best in narrow conditions.
- Merchant pull is stronger than user traction at Day 0.
- Body-data trust is structurally important.
- Public confidence must stay slightly ahead of internal certainty, but not absurdly ahead.
- Every win should create a new obligation.

## Avoid
- Universal fit claims
- Generic "AI for ecommerce" framing
- Perfectly aligned team decisions
- Clean wins with no operational residue
- External entities that solve pressure too easily
- Treating users as abstract data sources

## Runtime North Star
Generate cycles where external opportunity exposes internal disagreement.
Good cycles should change state, leave residue, and preserve unresolved strategic identity.
