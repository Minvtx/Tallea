# World Rules

## What This World Rewards
- Evidence that Tallea reduces sizing doubt in one concrete garment category, not vague claims about all fashion.
- Merchant proof tied to specific pain: fewer sizing questions, fewer exchanges, better checkout confidence, or clearer return reasons.
- Honest confidence language that protects user trust while still giving merchants measurable value.
- Clean translation of messy catalog data into usable fit signals.
- Warm distribution through regional ecommerce operators, apparel founders, and platform-adjacent contacts.
- Product decisions that preserve the user profile as an asset rather than turning it into disposable merchant data.

## What This World Punishes
- Treating a successful denim or fitted-womenswear pilot as proof of universal apparel fit.
- Hiding manual catalog cleanup behind language that implies full automation.
- Letting merchants shape the product around conversion while the public story remains user empowerment.
- Asking users for body data without making benefit, control, and limits immediately clear.
- Expanding into loose, stretchy, luxury, marketplace, or cross-border categories before the confidence model can handle them.
- Taking Argentine scarcity as permission to accept any deal that extends runway.

## What Changes Quickly
- Merchant urgency after a good demo or peer recommendation.
- Startup-scene interpretation of Tallea as consumer app, B2B tool, or infrastructure play.
- Roadmap priorities when a pilot comes with near-term revenue.
- Team confidence after one visible fit success or one humiliating recommendation miss.
- Public language after a newsletter mention, pitch event, or platform intro.
- Internal power around whoever controls merchant access, product truth, or technical credibility.

## What Changes Slowly
- User comfort with body-profile creation.
- True cross-brand fit reliability.
- Merchant catalog quality.
- Habitual consumer use outside a purchase moment.
- A defensible dataset connecting body profiles, garment attributes, recommendations, and outcomes.
- Internal consensus on whether Tallea is user-led, merchant-led, or infrastructure-led.

## What Counts as Progress
- **Public progress:** A clear demo, a known apparel pilot, a sharper website, and language that makes the problem obvious without overclaiming.
- **Internal progress:** Lower manual cleanup, better drop-off behavior in the profile flow, fewer low-confidence edge cases shown as confident, and cleaner merchant data ingestion.
- **Real but risky progress:** A paid pilot, white-label interest, early integration access, or a merchant dashboard that becomes more valuable than the shopper experience.
- **Seductive but misleading progress:** Press calling it "AI sizing," investors praising the infrastructure story before user trust exists, or one strong category result treated as platform proof.

## Typical Sources of Pressure
- Fit-sensitive merchants want help before Tallea has enough real-world validation.
- Users need the product to feel respectful before they will complete the body-profile flow.
- Runway pressure makes merchant revenue more tempting than slow consumer adoption.
- Catalog mess creates hidden operational cost after every promising sales conversation.
- Public clarity makes the company look more mature than its product boundary.
- Early ecosystem instruments most likely to matter: market validation, reputation, technical dependence, capital pressure, partner access, and sensitive information.
- Dangerous combinations: merchant pilot plus weak catalog data, public attention plus narrow product truth, white-label interest plus user-trust mission, runway pressure plus privacy shortcuts.

## Typical Failure Modes
- Tallea becomes a returns-reduction widget with a friendlier brand wrapper.
- One pilot brand’s catalog logic silently becomes the product’s default worldview.
- The team optimizes completion by making body-data consent too frictionless and later pays for it in trust.
- Manual cleanup and recommendation overrides remain invisible until a pilot asks to scale.
- A bad recommendation in denim, trousers, or fitted dresses turns a technical miss into an emotional trust event.
- The company confuses merchant enthusiasm with proof that shoppers want a reusable fit identity.

## Strategic Drift and Identity Risk
- Tallea can drift from "help shoppers know their fit" to "help merchants sell more" to "standardize apparel fit data for platforms."
- The most deforming opportunities are white-label widgets, marketplace integrations, return analytics, and dashboards that make user-facing value feel secondary.
- The hidden asset may be the structured fit dataset: body signals, garment metadata, confidence outcomes, exchange reasons, and merchant corrections.
- Success may create identity confusion when the website remains consumer-friendly while revenue, roadmap, and investor attention move B2B.
- The company should never resolve too early whether the reusable fit profile is a product, a data asset, a trust layer, or a distribution tactic.

## Consequence Logic
- Every merchant win should create operational residue: support load, data cleanup, integration maintenance, and pressure to show ROI.
- Every public claim about confidence should leave reputational residue if the product later fails in that exact area.
- Every improvement in automation should expose a new class of edge cases.
- Every privacy shortcut should create delayed trust risk, even if conversion improves immediately.
- Every pilot should shift internal politics toward whoever can claim credit for revenue, product truth, or user trust.
- Every external validation event should clarify one thing and make another more ambiguous.

## System Guidance
- **Preserve:** The useful core problem, the Argentine-origin operating pressure, the narrow initial wedge, and the public/internal split between shopper confidence and merchant value.
- **Keep unstable:** Product generality, strategic identity, privacy comfort, merchant dependence, and whether external validation should be trusted.
- **Track as state:** User beta completion, merchant pilot status, catalog cleanup burden, confidence accuracy by garment category, trust/privacy incidents, runway pressure, and public category interpretation.
- **Keep open at the beginning:** Whether Tallea becomes consumer trust infrastructure, merchant conversion software, fit-data infrastructure, or an acquisition target for a commerce platform.

## Summary for System Use
Tallea’s world rewards narrow proof, honest confidence, merchant-specific value, and the ability to turn messy apparel data into usable fit signals. It punishes premature generalization, hidden manual work, privacy shortcuts, and strategic splits between public user trust and merchant revenue. Early cycles should prioritize fitted-apparel pilots, catalog-data friction, body-profile trust, white-label temptation, and the three traction layers: users, merchants, and ecosystem legitimacy. Progress should almost always create new obligations rather than clean resolution.
