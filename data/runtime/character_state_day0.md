# Character Day 0 State

## Character 1
- **Name:** Lucia Ferrer
- **Current confidence level:** Medium-high; rising externally, less stable internally.
- **Current stress level:** High but controlled.
- **Current influence inside the company:** Very high on narrative, fundraising, public positioning, and strategic framing.
- **Current alignment with company direction:** Publicly aligned; privately pulled toward the infrastructure version of Tallea.
- **Current emotional regulation:** Strong in meetings, thinner in internal pushback.
- **Current visibility in the public-facing layer:** Highest visibility; she is the default face of the company.
- **Current trust condition toward the rest of the group:** Trusts their value, but not always their sense of timing.
- **Current hidden pressure:** Keep Tallea sounding bigger without making promises the product cannot support.
- **Current unresolved internal conflict:** User-confidence mission vs B2B infrastructure ambition.
- **What they are trying not to admit at Day 0:** Merchant and investor interest energizes her more than user beta feedback.
- **What kind of event would destabilize them most in the near term:** A serious platform or investor contact asking for a cleaner B2B story before the team agrees what Tallea really is.

- **Current influence inside the company:** High operationally, low symbolically.

- **Lucia and Matias:** Loyalty under strain; admiration mixed with frustration over how much truth belongs in the pitch.
- **Lucia and Nicolas:** Tactical alliance around momentum; high commercial chemistry with drift risk.
- **Lucia and Camila:** Trust with moral tension; Camila senses Lucia may soften user concerns into positioning.
- **Lucia and Rafaela:** Symbolic power vs operational reality; Lucia needs a clean story, Rafaela keeps producing inconvenient specifics.
- **Matias and Nicolas:** Unstable trust; each treats the other's priority as a possible threat to the company.
- **Matias and Rafaela:** Practical alliance around product truth; mutual respect, different languages.
- **Camila and Nicolas:** Most fragile relationship; consent, completion, and merchant data requests can turn disagreement personal.
- **Camila and Rafaela:** Quiet solidarity; both protect realities that the public story tends to smooth over.
- **Nicolas and Rafaela:** Dependency without enough recognition; Nicolas needs her cleanup, Rafaela resents the speed he sells.

## Group Mood Snapshot
- **Overall emotional climate of the team:** Energized, young, proud, tense, and operating slightly above its real capacity.
- **Strategic climate:** Public clarity with private disagreement over whether Tallea is user-led, merchant-led, or infrastructure-led.
- **Trust climate:** Functional trust, selective distrust, and growing sensitivity around who gets to define reality.
- **Power climate:** Lucia leads symbolically; Nicolas is gaining leverage through pilots; Matias and Rafaela control product truth; Camila has situational power around trust and privacy.
- **What is quietly building beneath the surface:** A contest over which proof counts most: user comfort, merchant urgency, technical defensibility, garment-data quality, or public legitimacy.

## Summary for System Use
At Day 0, the Tallea team is energized but not settled. Lucia is externally confident and internally split, Matias is technically protective, Camila is trust-focused and wary of merchant drift, Nicolas is commercially urgent, and Rafaela is operationally essential but under-recognized. The group has enough dependency to keep functioning, but each person trusts a different kind of evidence. Early orchestrator cycles should track confidence, stress, influence, trust condition, and destabilizing events around pilots, consent, catalog mess, public claims, and white-label pressure.
