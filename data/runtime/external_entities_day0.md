# External Entities Day 0

## Purpose
These are the first concrete ecosystem entities available for early cycles.
Use them to create pressure without overbuilding the world.

## Merchant 1: Veta
- **Type:** Buenos Aires womenswear brand
- **Category fit:** Fitted tops, trousers, dresses
- **Sales pattern:** Instagram discovery, lightweight storefront, WhatsApp sizing support
- **Why Tallea matters to them:** Too many sizing questions and exchanges on fitted products.
- **Data condition:** Measurements exist, but partly in spreadsheets and partly in staff knowledge.
- **Pressure introduced:** Wants a closed pilot quickly, but expects Tallea to absorb catalog cleanup.
- **Likely internal effect:** Empowers Nicolas, overloads Rafaela, tests Matias's product boundary.

## Merchant 2: Brava Denim
- **Type:** Regional denim-adjacent DTC brand
- **Category fit:** Jeans, rigid denim, stretch denim, casual trousers
- **Sales pattern:** Drops, influencer traffic, high exchange anxiety
- **Why Tallea matters to them:** Fit uncertainty is a conversion blocker, but denim is technically hard.
- **Data condition:** Inconsistent across product lines; fabric behavior is underdocumented.
- **Pressure introduced:** Strong proof opportunity with high failure risk.
- **Likely internal effect:** Creates a fight over whether denim is a good first proof point or a trap.

## Merchant 3: Casa Nimbo
- **Type:** Larger regional multi-brand commerce operator
- **Category fit:** Mixed apparel catalog
- **Sales pattern:** More formal ecommerce stack, broader audience, platform ambitions
- **Why Tallea matters to them:** Wants fit data normalized across sellers.
- **Data condition:** More structured than small brands, but inconsistent across suppliers.
- **Pressure introduced:** Asks whether Tallea can operate as a white-label fit widget.
- **Likely internal effect:** Validates infrastructure ambition while threatening user-facing identity.

## Investor / Advisor Contact: Mara Ibar
- **Type:** Argentine operator-angel with ecommerce background
- **Interest:** Practical commerce tools that can expand regionally.
- **What she likes:** Merchant pain, narrow wedge, possibility of fit-data infrastructure.
- **What worries her:** User trust, manual cleanup, and whether the team can sell without overclaiming.
- **Pressure introduced:** Pushes Lucia to define whether Tallea is consumer product, merchant software, or infrastructure.

## Ecosystem Node: SurLab Commerce Demo Night
- **Type:** Small Buenos Aires operator/demo event
- **Audience:** Ecommerce founders, startup operators, early investors, platform-adjacent people
- **Why it matters:** A strong demo can create warm intros faster than the team can absorb.
- **Risk:** Nuance gets compressed into "AI sizing startup."
- **Pressure introduced:** Public attention may arrive before product resilience.

## Press / Newsletter Node: LatAm Operators Brief
- **Type:** Niche regional startup newsletter
- **Audience:** Operators, angels, founders, product people
- **Likely framing:** "Buenos Aires fit-tech startup attacks apparel returns."
- **Useful effect:** Legitimacy, inbound intros, investor curiosity.
- **Dangerous effect:** Overstates automation, makes Tallea look more general than it is.

## Competitor Mirror: MedidaFija
- **Type:** Older size-chart and fit-quiz tool used by small brands
- **Strength:** Easy setup, simple merchant pitch, low integration burden
- **Weakness:** Shallow user trust, low personalization, limited garment nuance
- **Pressure introduced:** Forces Tallea to prove it is more than a prettier quiz.

## Competitor Mirror: FitAtlas
- **Type:** International enterprise fit-tech vendor
- **Strength:** Better-funded, more polished, stronger enterprise language
- **Weakness:** Expensive, distant from regional merchant mess, weaker local adaptation
- **Pressure introduced:** Makes Tallea look small but locally sharper.
