# World Constitution

## Purpose
This document defines the narrative constitution of the world as a generative system.

It sets the structural laws that govern world behavior before specific protagonists, a central group, or named characters exist.

It does not define:

- characters
- archetypes
- the protagonist core

It defines:

- how pressure is produced
- how cost operates
- how the world progresses
- how consequences accumulate
- how public perception works
- how the environment moves
- how serial continuity is sustained

---

## 1. Nothing Sustains Itself

**Rule**
- The natural state of the world is active fragility.
- Nothing important remains functional, relevant, or legitimate without continuous cost.

**What this means**
- Stability is never permanent.
- Every structure requires maintenance.
- Every achieved position requires defense, adaptation, or replenishment.
- Without intervention, deterioration advances.

**Why it matters**
- It creates baseline urgency without artificial crisis.
- It makes continuity itself narratively valuable.
- It prevents achievements from becoming inert decoration.

**Use**
- Model passive erosion of resources, position, or viability.
- Make every advantage carry maintenance cost.
- Let success create new obligations.
- Penalize prolonged inaction without making the system unplayable.

**Avoid**
- a world so fragile that everything feels doomed from the start

---

## 2. Every Decision Happens Under Pressure

**Rule**
- Important decisions should happen under constraint, deadline, exposure, dependence, or scarcity.

**What this means**
- The world does not provide neutral decision spaces.
- Something is always missing, expiring, watching, or at risk.

**Why it matters**
- Pressure compresses options and reveals sacrifice.
- It prevents the narrative from stalling in abstract deliberation.

**Use**
- Keep multiple pressure axes active: time, resources, legitimacy, access, validation, reputation, operational capacity.
- Make relevant decisions involve trade-offs.
- Let solved pressure shift form instead of removing friction entirely.
- Separate immediate, medium-term, and latent pressures.

**Avoid**
- constant maximum urgency

---

## 3. Every Advance Reconfigures the System

**Rule**
- No achievement closes the world.
- Every improvement changes the balance, raises expectations, creates obligations, and introduces new vulnerabilities.

**What this means**
- Progress is real, but never final.
- Each gain changes the nature of the next problem.

**Why it matters**
- It allows real growth without exhausting the story.
- Complexity expands as progress happens.

**Use**
- Attach emergent consequences to each victory.
- Redefine the environment after major milestones.
- Raise standards, exposure, or dependence after progress.
- Do not let resolution leave the board unchanged.

**Avoid**
- turning every success into immediate punishment

---

## 4. The World Responds Indirectly

**Rule**
- Actions do not reliably produce the exact result they were meant to produce.
- The world responds through chains, multiple actors, incomplete information, and domain interdependence.

**What this means**
- Intention does not fully control outcome.
- Decisions can create side effects, mixed outcomes, delays, or reversals without breaking system logic.

**Why it matters**
- It creates coherent surprise, irony, deviation, and causal complexity.
- It prevents a linear and predictable narrative.

**Use**
- Make effects depend on context, not only intention.
- Allow oblique, partial, or displaced outcomes.
- Propagate consequences across technical, reputational, financial, institutional, operational, and social domains.
- Reserve clean alignment for rare moments of high competence or unusual clarity.

**Avoid**
- a world where intention and outcome never align

---

## 5. The Past Never Disappears

**Rule**
- Important actions leave causal residue.
- The world accumulates memory, debt, reputation, precedent, hidden costs, and delayed consequences.

**What this means**
- Nothing important fully resets.
- Past events alter thresholds, interpretation, access, predisposition, and future risk.

**Why it matters**
- It creates serial continuity, historical depth, and accumulated biography.
- Decisions keep weight beyond the cycle in which they happen.

**Use**
- Track pending consequences.
- Activate effects in the short, medium, and long term.
- Turn some events into persistent world modifiers.
- Let history change how new events are read.

**Avoid**
- too much unresolved debt at once

---

## 6. The World Exceeds the Narrative Focus

**Rule**
- The world does not exist only for what is currently at the center of the story.
- Institutions, outside actors, markets, reputation systems, and structural forces have their own agendas and rhythms.

**What this means**
- The environment keeps moving even when the focal group does not act.
- Off-screen shifts eventually affect what becomes visible.

**Why it matters**
- It creates the feeling of a living ecosystem.
- It prevents the world from seeming built only to answer the protagonists.

**Use**
- Model external actors with autonomous goals.
- Execute state changes outside the narrative center.
- Allow interactions between external forces without immediate relation to the focus.
- Let some changes arrive indirectly or late.

**Avoid**
- an outside world so intense or arbitrary that it erases local agency

---

## 7. Public Perception Never Fully Matches Reality

**Rule**
- There is a structural gap between what actually happens and what circulates, gets interpreted, or is believed.

**What this means**
- Public perception simplifies, amplifies, distorts, dramatizes, or minimizes complex processes.
- That perception still has real material effects.

**Why it matters**
- It multiplies conflict layers.
- It enables tension between operational truth and public image.

**Use**
- Maintain one internal reality layer and one external perception layer.
- Let both influence each other without fully matching.
- Treat reputation, coverage, and rumor as variables with real effects.
- Allow image-management attempts with uncertain outcomes.

**Avoid**
- completely arbitrary public perception

---

## 8. Complexity Must Become Legible

**Rule**
- The world may be complex, but its stakes must still be felt.
- Structural abstractions should translate into tangible consequences, clear dilemmas, visible limits, or perceptible risk.

**What this means**
- Full system understanding is not required for something to matter.
- Complexity cannot block the experience of conflict.

**Why it matters**
- It keeps complexity from becoming cold, opaque, or inert.
- It keeps drama accessible without flattening the world.

**Use**
- Translate abstract processes into observable effects.
- Turn structural pressure into concrete choices or visible losses.
- Signal gravity through consequence, reaction, cost, or urgency.
- Deliver explanation inside situations with stakes.

**Avoid**
- oversimplification
- unreadable abstraction

---

## 9. Information Is Always Unevenly Distributed

**Rule**
- No part of the world has access to the full system at the same time.
- Information moves partially, asymmetrically, late, or through interested actors.

**What this means**
- Conflict can emerge from ignorance, secrecy, leaks, incomplete interpretation, or delayed revelation.
- Knowing something earlier or later changes power and risk.

**Why it matters**
- It enables anticipation, error, maneuvering, dramatic irony, and justified surprise.
- Understanding the world becomes part of the conflict itself.

**Use**
- Distribute information by access, position, and interest.
- Control who knows what, when, and with what level of certainty.
- Alternate moments where the system, the focus, or the audience knows more, less, or something different.
- Use revelation as an event with consequences, not just clarification.

**Avoid**
- arbitrary concealment with no structural basis

---

## 10. Each Cycle Must Close Something and Open Something Else

**Rule**
- Each serial unit should mix resolution, reconfiguration, and new pressure.

**What this means**
- No cycle should end in total emptiness or total closure.
- Each cycle should leave some combination of progress, cost, residue, or vulnerability.

**Why it matters**
- It sustains serial continuity.
- It gives enough reward to justify the cycle and enough hunger to return.

**Use**
- Design each cycle with one main resolution and one secondary opening.
- Close one question while activating another of a different kind.
- Combine partial relief with board reconfiguration.
- Use poisoned wins, false resolutions, or partially contained spirals sometimes, but not as the only formula.

**Avoid**
- cycles that close nothing
- cycles that close everything

---

## General Design Standard

These laws are structural principles, not rigid formulas.

The goal is not to maximize chaos or suffering.
The goal is to produce a world that:

- feels alive
- sustains pressure without exhausting itself
- allows progress without closure
- accumulates memory without paralysis
- generates continuity through real consequences
